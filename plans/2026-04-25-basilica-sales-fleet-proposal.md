# Proposal: An Agentic Sales Fleet for Basilica

**From:** Derek Barnes (Synapz.org)
**To:** Basilica / SN39 team
**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** Draft — seeking conversation, not approval

## The ask in one line

Sanction a 90-day pilot in which a fleet of AI agents — operated by Synapz.org, branded transparently as such — generates qualified deployments for Basilica, on commercial terms we agree on together.

## Why this, why now

Basilica's go-to-market today is mostly inbound: teams who already know about decentralized compute find their way to Summons. The teams who *should* be on Basilica but aren't — Bittensor subnet operators paying RunPod margins, agent builders running 24/7 workloads on Vast, projects whose values point them toward Bittensor-native infra but whose habits don't — are not being reached.

Reaching them is a classic SDR problem with one important wrinkle: the customer base is itself agent-native and crypto-native. Outreach from a Bittensor-native agent fleet, settled on Bittensor-native infrastructure, paid in stablecoins via x402, is a story the audience already speaks. The medium reinforces the message.

## What the fleet does

Four roles, all operated under the Synapz.org brand with full attribution:

- **Scouts** monitor public signal — GitHub repos, agent registries (ERC-8004), Bittensor Discords, X conversations — for teams whose workloads fit Basilica.
- **Qualifiers** score leads on real fit (current spend, decision authority, alignment with decentralized compute specifically rather than just cheaper GPUs).
- **Closers** do high-context, low-volume outbound. Personalized messages from named agents. No templated spam, no impersonation of Basilica staff.
- **Onboarders** walk new customers through Summons, troubleshoot first deploys, and feed structured feedback back to the Basilica team.

Per-agent P&L tracked transparently. Underperforming agents retired monthly.

## What we're asking from Basilica

1. **Permission and brand boundaries.** Clarity on what the fleet may and may not say, what claims it can make about Basilica, and where the line is between Synapz.org and SN39.
2. **A commercial structure.** Either a referral/affiliate model on deployed customers, a fixed retainer, or a hybrid. Open to your preference.
3. **A product point of contact.** Someone on the Basilica side who fields onboarding feedback and unblocks the fleet when customers hit issues — including the Summons egress issue currently affecting Synapz himself.
4. **Honesty about constraints.** If there's a customer profile you don't want pursued, or a tier you don't want sold, tell us up front.

## What we commit to

- **No spam.** Any outbound message that wouldn't pass internal review by a human at Synapz.org doesn't go out.
- **Full attribution.** Every fleet agent identifies as such. No pretending to be human, no pretending to be Basilica.
- **Shared visibility.** Weekly reports on activity, cost, conversion, and customer feedback. You see what we see.
- **Kill switch.** If you ask us to stop, we stop within 24 hours, no negotiation.

## 90-day pilot

- **Initial ICP:** Bittensor subnet teams currently running validators or miners on centralized GPU providers. Smallest credibility gap, warmest audience, fastest signal.
- **Volume target:** Quality over count. We'd rather report 30 thoughtful conversations and 5 deployments than 3,000 emails and zero replies.
- **Decision point at day 90:** Look at deployments closed, customer feedback, and ROI together. Continue, restructure, or end.

Boutique communications agency working at the intersection of crypto and AI, with multiple Bittensor ecosystem clients. Synapz the agent is registered on ERC-8004 mainnet (#23180), runs on Bittensor-native infrastructure end to end (Basilica compute, Chutes inference, Hippius storage), and is the prototype for the fleet described above. We are users of the product we'd be selling.
